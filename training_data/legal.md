# Legal Specialist — Agricultural Law Reference

## Federal Agricultural Regulations

### Farm Bill Overview
- Omnibus legislation reauthorized approximately every five years (current: Agriculture Improvement Act of 2018)
- Title I — Commodity Programs: Price Loss Coverage (PLC) and Agriculture Risk Coverage (ARC) for eligible crops
- Title II — Conservation: Conservation Reserve Program (CRP), Environmental Quality Incentives Program (EQIP), Conservation Stewardship Program (CSP)
- Title IV — Nutrition: Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) funding
- Title XI — Crop Insurance: Federal Crop Insurance Act provisions, Revenue Protection (RP) and Yield Protection (YP) policies
- Producers must enroll and make ARC/PLC elections on a commodity-by-commodity, farm-by-farm basis each program year

### USDA Programs
- Farm Service Agency (FSA): commodity loans, disaster assistance (ELAP, LFP, LIP), farm ownership and operating loans
- Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS): technical assistance, cost-share for conservation practices
- Risk Management Agency (RMA): administers federal crop insurance
- USDA organic certification through accredited certifying agents; National Organic Program (NOP) standards under 7 CFR Part 205
- Beginning Farmer and Rancher programs: reduced interest rates, down-payment loans, targeted set-asides

### Conservation Compliance
- Highly Erodible Land Conservation (HELC): producers must apply approved conservation systems on highly erodible land to remain eligible for USDA benefits
- Wetland Conservation (Swampbuster): converting wetlands to cropland triggers loss of USDA program eligibility
- Conservation plans developed with local NRCS offices; variances require formal approval
- Violations may result in partial or full benefit repayment

## EPA Regulations Affecting Agriculture

### Clean Water Act (CWA)
- Section 402 NPDES permits required for concentrated animal feeding operations (CAFOs) that discharge pollutants to waters of the United States
- Section 404 permits required for dredge-and-fill activities in wetlands and jurisdictional waters
- Agricultural stormwater runoff and return flows from irrigated agriculture are generally exempt from NPDES permitting
- "Waters of the United States" (WOTUS) definition has been subject to ongoing regulatory revision; check current rule

### FIFRA — Pesticide Regulations
- Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act governs registration, labeling, sale, and use of pesticides
- Applicators must follow label directions; the label is the law
- Restricted-use pesticides (RUPs) require a certified applicator license
- Worker Protection Standard (40 CFR Part 170): training, notification, restricted-entry intervals (REIs), personal protective equipment for agricultural workers and handlers
- Record-keeping requirements for RUP applications: date, location, product, rate, acreage

### CERCLA (Superfund)
- Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability Act imposes strict, joint-and-several liability for releases of hazardous substances
- Agricultural operations may face liability for historical pesticide contamination, underground storage tanks, or waste disposal
- CERCLA exempts normal application of registered pesticides but not spills or improper disposal
- Fair Act of 2018 exempts air emissions from animal waste from CERCLA and EPCRA reporting

## Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA)

- Signed 2011; shifts FDA approach from response to prevention
- Produce Safety Rule (21 CFR Part 112): science-based standards for growing, harvesting, packing, and holding of fruits and vegetables
  - Covers agricultural water quality, biological soil amendments, worker health/hygiene, domesticated and wild animals, equipment/tools
  - Qualified exemption for farms with less than $500,000 in average annual food sales (adjusted for inflation) that sell majority direct to consumers or local retailers
- Preventive Controls for Human Food (21 CFR Part 117): requires hazard analysis and risk-based preventive controls for food facilities
- Foreign Supplier Verification Program (FSVP): importers must verify foreign suppliers meet U.S. food safety standards
- Intentional Adulteration Rule: facilities must address vulnerabilities to intentional contamination
- FSMA does not generally apply to farms that only grow crops for animal feed, though the Preventive Controls for Animal Food rule covers feed mills

## OSHA Agricultural Exemptions and Requirements

- Small farm exemption: farms with 10 or fewer employees are exempt from most OSHA regulations and record-keeping (29 CFR 1928)
- Exemptions do NOT apply to temporary labor camps or operations with a temporary labor camp
- Field sanitation standard (29 CFR 1928.110): potable drinking water, toilet facilities, hand-washing within a quarter mile of workers; applies to farms with 11+ employees
- Tractor rollover protection (ROPS) required on tractors manufactured after October 25, 1976, used by employees
- Hazard Communication Standard applies to farms using hazardous chemicals
- Logging operations on farms are NOT exempt and must comply with 29 CFR 1910.266
- Grain handling standard (29 CFR 1910.272): applies to grain elevators, including farm-based elevators with 1 million+ bushels storage

## Water Rights Frameworks

### Prior Appropriation Doctrine
- Predominant in western states (Colorado, Wyoming, Montana, Idaho, Nevada, Utah, Arizona, New Mexico)
- "First in time, first in right" — senior rights holders have priority during shortages
- Rights acquired by beneficial use and may be lost through non-use (abandonment or forfeiture)
- Water rights are typically separate from land ownership and can be transferred or sold
- Must obtain a permit or decree before appropriating water; change-of-use applications required for new purposes

### Riparian Rights Doctrine
- Predominant in eastern states
- Landowners adjacent to a water body have a right to reasonable use of the water
- Rights are tied to land ownership and generally cannot be sold separately
- "Reasonable use" balances competing demands; no absolute priority
- Many eastern states now overlay a permit system on riparian rights (regulated riparianism)

### Hybrid Systems
- California, Texas, and several other states use hybrid systems combining elements of both doctrines
- Groundwater rights may follow different rules than surface water in the same state (e.g., rule of capture for groundwater in Texas)

## Agricultural Labor Law

### FLSA Agricultural Exemptions
- Section 13(a)(6): farms using fewer than 500 person-days of agricultural labor in any calendar quarter of the preceding year are exempt from federal minimum wage
- Family members of the farmer are exempt regardless of farm size
- Agricultural employees are exempt from federal overtime requirements (Section 13(b)(12))
- Child labor exceptions: children of any age may work on family farms; minors 12+ may work on small farms with parental consent
- The "agriculture" definition under FLSA is narrower than everyday usage; processing and packing may not qualify

### H-2A Temporary Agricultural Worker Program
- Employer must demonstrate temporary or seasonal need and a shortage of domestic workers
- Adverse Effect Wage Rate (AEWR) sets the minimum wage; varies by state and is updated annually
- Employer must provide free housing meeting federal standards and transportation from the point of hire
- 50% rule: employer must hire qualified domestic applicants until 50% of the contract period has elapsed
- Three-fourths guarantee: employer must offer at least 75% of total hours promised in the work contract
- Compliance enforced by Department of Labor Wage and Hour Division

## Business Entity Options for Farms

### Sole Proprietorship
- Simplest form; no formal filing required (other than assumed name registration in some states)
- Owner has unlimited personal liability for farm debts and legal claims
- Income reported on Schedule F (Form 1040); subject to self-employment tax

### Limited Liability Company (LLC)
- Provides personal liability protection for members
- Flexible management and profit-sharing structures via operating agreement
- Default tax treatment: single-member LLC taxed as sole proprietorship; multi-member as partnership
- May elect S-Corp or C-Corp taxation
- State annual filing fees and franchise taxes vary

### S-Corporation
- Limited liability with pass-through taxation (no entity-level federal income tax)
- Shareholders limited to 100; must be U.S. citizens or residents; one class of stock only
- Reasonable salary requirement for shareholder-employees reduces self-employment tax exposure
- Not ideal for holding appreciated farmland due to built-in gains tax on conversion from C-Corp

### Partnership (General and Limited)
- General partnership: all partners share management and unlimited liability
- Limited partnership: general partners manage and bear liability; limited partners are passive investors with liability limited to their investment
- Family limited partnerships (FLPs) used for estate planning and asset protection; subject to IRS scrutiny on valuation discounts

### Trusts
- Revocable living trusts avoid probate and provide management continuity
- Irrevocable trusts can reduce estate tax exposure but relinquish grantor control
- Grantor Retained Annuity Trusts (GRATs) and Intentionally Defective Grantor Trusts (IDGTs) used for transferring appreciating farm assets
- Dynasty trusts available in some states for multi-generational wealth transfer

## Agricultural Contract Essentials

### Production Contracts
- Common in poultry and livestock; grower provides labor and facilities, integrator provides inputs and retains ownership of animals
- Payment typically based on performance metrics (feed conversion, mortality)
- GIPSA (now Packers and Stockyards Division) regulations require fair dealing and prohibit retaliation
- Grower Investment Protection Act provisions in some states require disclosure of capital investment risks

### Marketing Contracts
- Producer retains ownership until delivery; price determined by formula, cash market, or futures-based pricing
- Forward contracts lock in price but carry delivery obligation and potential basis risk
- Understand force majeure clauses, quality specifications, and dispute resolution provisions

### Land Leases
- Cash rent leases: fixed annual payment; tenant bears production risk
- Crop-share leases: landlord and tenant split production and sometimes expenses
- Flex leases: base rent plus bonus tied to yields or prices
- Written leases should address term, renewal, improvements, conservation obligations, and termination notice (state-specific statutory notice periods apply)
- Right of first refusal clauses for purchase of leased land

## Estate Planning and Succession

- Federal estate tax exemption: $13.61 million per individual (2024, indexed for inflation); scheduled to sunset to approximately $7 million in 2026 absent legislative action
- Stepped-up basis at death eliminates capital gains on inherited assets; critical for land-heavy farm estates
- Section 2032A special use valuation: qualifying farm real property valued based on agricultural use rather than highest-and-best-use; can reduce taxable estate by up to $1.35 million (2024, adjusted annually)
- Section 6166 installment payment of estate tax: allows deferral over 14 years for estates where farm/business assets exceed 35% of adjusted gross estate
- Buy-sell agreements funded by life insurance ensure liquidity and orderly transfer
- Succession planning should address both ownership transfer and management transition; begin conversations 5-10 years before anticipated transition
- Annual gift tax exclusion ($18,000 per donee, 2024) for gradual transfer of interests
- Consider state-level estate or inheritance taxes; thresholds vary significantly (e.g., Oregon at $1 million vs. federal $13.61 million)

## Common State-Level Regulatory Areas

### Right-to-Farm Laws
- All 50 states have enacted right-to-farm statutes protecting established agricultural operations from nuisance lawsuits
- Protections generally require the operation to have been in existence before surrounding land-use changes
- Scope varies: some states protect against only private nuisance claims; others include public nuisance and local ordinances
- Operations must comply with applicable laws and accepted agricultural practices to maintain protection

### Nutrient Management
- Many states require Comprehensive Nutrient Management Plans (CNMPs) for livestock operations above threshold sizes
- Manure application rates based on soil testing and crop nutrient uptake; nitrogen and phosphorus limits
- Some states require certified nutrient management planners
- Annual reporting and record-keeping requirements vary by state

### Pesticide Licensing
- State departments of agriculture administer pesticide applicator certification under EPA-approved plans
- Private applicator certification for farmers applying RUPs on their own or employer's land
- Commercial applicator certification for custom applicators; additional categories and examinations
- Continuing education credits (CEUs) required for recertification; cycle length varies (typically 3-5 years)
- State-specific pesticide registration, restricted-use designations, and buffer zone requirements may exceed federal standards

## Tax Law Highlights

### Section 1031 Like-Kind Exchanges
- Allows deferral of capital gains tax when exchanging real property held for productive use in a trade or business
- Since the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017, applies only to real property (not equipment, livestock, or other personal property)
- 45-day identification period and 180-day closing deadline from sale of relinquished property
- Qualified intermediary must hold proceeds; taxpayer cannot have constructive receipt
- Common in farmland transactions to consolidate or reposition holdings

### Installment Sales (Section 453)
- Allows spreading gain recognition over the payment period when sale price is received in installments
- Useful for retiring farmers selling to the next generation to manage tax brackets
- Related-party sales subject to special rules and potential acceleration of gain if buyer resells within two years
- Depreciation recapture (Section 1245/1250) is recognized in full in the year of sale, not deferred

### Conservation Easements
- Qualified conservation contributions under Section 170(h) provide income tax deductions for the value of the easement
- Enhanced deduction for qualified farmers and ranchers: up to 100% of AGI (vs. 50% for non-farmers) with 15-year carryforward
- Easement must be granted to a qualified land trust or government entity in perpetuity
- Must serve a valid conservation purpose: habitat preservation, open space, historic preservation, or agricultural use
- IRS has increased scrutiny of syndicated conservation easement transactions; ensure independent qualified appraisal
- Easements reduce property value and may affect borrowing capacity; coordinate with lenders

### Additional Agricultural Tax Provisions
- Section 179 expensing and bonus depreciation for farm equipment and qualifying improvements
- Income averaging for farmers (Schedule J): allows spreading current-year income over three prior years to reduce marginal tax rate
- Prepaid farm supplies: generally deductible in the year of payment if not exceeding 50% of other deductible farm expenses
- Commodity Credit Corporation (CCC) loan election: option to report loan proceeds as income in the year received rather than when the commodity is sold
- Fuel tax credits for farming use of gasoline and diesel (Form 4136)
